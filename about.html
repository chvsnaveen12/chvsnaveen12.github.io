About me

I am Naveen Chavali(in red) a student of 12th and a guy who just likes to tinker. I have worked with C++, high performance C, Python, Micro-controllers (using register accesses), x86 assembly, Web-Dev, CAD and 3d-Printers. Due to the pandemic, I haven't done much in these past 2 years, but am trying to change that as soon as possible. If you'd like to reach out, feel free.

I was always fascinated by things growing up. I would rip open everything in my sight and try to understand how they work, but couldn't as they were always too complex. I used to watch people build electronic circuits effortlessly using complicated chips and components. I was too confused, I didn't even know where to start. It all changed when a teacher, came to our society and offered up classes for practical electronics. We used to not let him go even till 9PM even if the class should have ended at 7PM. These classes made me gradually comfortable with electronics. After 1-2 years i was able to design and manufacture my own designs. Then I found Ben-Eater on Youtube and absolutely got addicted to his videos. I always used to wonder how a computer functioned from the inside, we were told in school that a CPU has an Accumalator, an ALU, some Registers etc. But how did they all work? I finally understood 1980s computing standards due to Ben-Eater and MITOCW and built a couple of computers equivalent to the 1980s standards. And I am trying to continue to grow in this field.

Have I donr things only related to CS? Currently I am interested in hardware design, but that doesn't mean I am not trying other things as well. I have also tried Computational Fluid Dynamics, Hydroponics, CAD and Ultra-sonic arrays, although at a much lower level
